### Basic Heading Hierarchy

Define the semantic level of your headings using the element prop to structure your content correctly for accessibility and SEO.

```tsx
<Heading element="h1">CloudHop Platform (H1)</Heading>
<Heading element="h2">Communication Evolved (H2)</Heading>
<Heading element="h3">HopHub Channels (H3)</Heading>
<Heading element="h4">Direct Messages (H4)</Heading>
<Heading element="h5">Online Users (H5)</Heading>
<Heading element="h6">Settings (H6)</Heading>
```

### Customizing Visual Appearance

Use the visualLevel prop to apply different typographic styles like display or body sizes independent of the semantic element. This allows for flexible design while maintaining document structure.

```tsx
<Heading element="h1" visualLevel="display-large">
  Experience the Nebula
</Heading>
<Heading element="h2" visualLevel="display-medium">
  Unified Digital Space
</Heading>
<Heading element="h3" visualLevel="display-small">
  Seamless Connectivity
</Heading>
<Heading element="h4" visualLevel="body-large">
  Important Notice
</Heading>
<Heading element="h5" visualLevel="body-medium">
  Sub-section detail
</Heading>
<Heading element="h6" visualLevel="h3">
  Another Heading
</Heading>
```

### Inverse Color for Dark Backgrounds

Apply the inverseColor prop when placing headings on dark backgrounds or nebula-themed sections to ensure optimal readability and visual contrast.

```tsx
<div
  style={{
    padding: '48px',
    backgroundColor: 'var(--colors-surface-nebula)',
    background: 'var(--effects-gradients-nebula)',
    borderRadius: 'var(--borders-radius-large)',
  }}
>
  <Heading element="h2" visualLevel="display-small" inverseColor>
    Welcome to HopHub
  </Heading>
  <Heading element="h3" visualLevel="h4" inverseColor style={{ opacity: 0.9 }}>
    Connect with your community in real-time, share moments, and explore the galaxy of content.
  </Heading>
</div>
